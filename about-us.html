Professor Zumbi

Professor Zumbi (Mamadu) was born in Guinea-Bissau, West Africa, and spent most of his childhood there until he moved to Lisbon. He first came into contact with the world of capoeira in 2008, a year after moving to London. His nickname “Zumbi” is the name of an ancient capoeira warrior, who fought against slavery. Mamadu enjoys playing the traditional instruments of capoeira, especially the atabaque drum. Mamadu has experience teaching all levels of adults, and has participated in a variety of performances and TV shows.

Capoeira Club Locations

London

Capoeira Club London was created in 2012, by Mestre Hiram Ribeiro. CCL now has more than 500 students and has expanded to schools and gyms all over the city, bringing capoeira to children and adults. The Club has a team of skilled teachers, led by Mestre Hiram, who between them give around 40 classes a week to all ages and all abilities.

Capoeira Club London

Essex

Capoeira Club Essex was founded by Rui De Sousa (Professor Molinha). Our services include classes, school clubs, one-to-one coaching, birthday parties, and high-energy performances. If you want to improve your fitness, learn acrobatics, music skills and self-defence, come and give it a try!

Croydon

Join Capoeira Club Croydon every Friday from 7:15 to 8:30 for a fun and energizing capoeira class with a Brazilian music aerobic warm up - run by Professor Caline Cezimbra.
