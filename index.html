What is Capoeira?

Origins

Capoeira is an Afro-Brazilian martial art that emerged in colonial Brazil during the 1600s, out of the struggle enslaved African and indigenous people faced for freedom. They not only practiced it for survival and to fight against their oppressors, but also to pass on traditions and spiritual practices from their different cultures. It was disguised as a dance or ritual to prevent its detection by the colonial authorities.

Modern Capoeira

After the abolition of slavery in Brazil in 1888, capoeira was illegal and its practitioners were socially ostracized for more than 40 years. Mestre Bimba, the legendary Capoeira Master, rescued the art form and proved its legitimacy, opening capoeira's first official school in Bahia, Brazil, in 1932.

Martial Art

In a capoeira game, players combine kicks and sometimes takedowns, with playful acrobatics and dance-like moves to catch their opponent off guard - it is important to always keep moving!

Music

The capoeira game is played in a 'roda' or 'circle' of people, adding to the energy and rhythm of the game by clapping their hands, singing capoeira songs in Portugese, and playing instruments.

Community

There are now many different capoeira schools that have formed networks around the world, with a strong community here in the UK. Increasingly visible in popular culture and mainstream media, Capoeira has gained respect as a martial art form and continues to inspire and bring people together.
